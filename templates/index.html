Allergy
An allergy is an immune system reaction to a foreign substance that's not typically harmful to your body. These foreign substances are called allergens. They can include certain foods, pollen, or pet dander. Your immune system's job is to keep you healthy by fighting harmful pathogens.

Medications

  • Antihistamines (Cetirizine, Loratadine)
  • Decongestants (Pseudoephedrine)
  • Nasal corticosteroids
  • Leukotriene modifiers
  • Allergy shots (Immunotherapy)

Precautions

  • Identify and avoid allergy triggers
  • Keep windows closed during high pollen
  • Use HEPA air purifiers
  • Wear a mask when cleaning or outdoors
  • Wash bedding weekly in hot water

Diet Recommendations

  • Foods rich in Vitamin C (citrus fruits)
  • Omega-3 fatty acids (fish, flaxseeds)
  • Local honey (for pollen allergies)
  • Probiotic-rich foods (yogurt, kefir)
  • Stay hydrated with plenty of water

Workout Suggestions

  • Indoor exercises during high pollen
  • Swimming in chlorinated pools
  • Morning workouts (lower pollen)
  • Yoga for stress relief and breathing
  • Use treadmill or stationary bike indoors
